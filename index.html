My Journey in Tech

From Sci-Fi Enthusiast to Self-Taught Technologist: My Journey into the Digital World

As a child, I was captivated by the futuristic worlds of sci-fi movies. The idea of manipulating vast amounts of data, building intelligent systems, and crafting digital solutions fascinated me. I saw myself in those tech-savvy characters—problem solvers who could shape the future with their skills. That vision stayed with me.

Originally, my dream was to study aviation and become a pilot, but financial constraints, limited scholarship opportunities and lack of full information made it an unattainable goal. However, I had always believed that technology was the ultimate equalizer—a field where skill, determination, and self-learning could outweigh formal credentials. If I couldn’t take to the skies, I would take on the digital frontier.

From Education Admin to Tech Innovator

My professional journey began in the education sector, where I served as an Education Assistant in ICT at Sitoromo Primary School. In this role, I quickly became a key player in integrating technology into classrooms, optimizing learning management systems (LMS), and ensuring the effective utilization of ICT resources. I took ownership of data management, IT troubleshooting, and system audits, ensuring that educators and students had the digital tools they needed to thrive.

This experience not only sharpened my technical problem-solving skills but also helped me develop business acumen, allowing me to bridge the gap between technology and operational efficiency. It was here that I realized the immense potential of programming—how a few lines of code could automate tasks, optimize workflows, and solve real-world challenges.

Becoming a Self-Taught Programmer

Determined to dive deeper into the tech world, I embarked on an intensive self-learning journey. Since formal education was not an option, I turned to free and open-source resources, enrolling in rigorous online programs that helped me develop my skills in software engineering, web development, and data analysis.

I achieved several key certifications that solidified my expertise:

  • PCEP – Certified Entry-Level Python Programmer (2024)
    Developed a strong foundation in Python, focusing on scripting, automation, and data structures. Built real-world applications, optimizing workflows and improving system efficiency.
  • Web Development (SheCodes, 2024)
    Mastered HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, allowing me to create responsive, interactive web applications. Gained exposure to front-end and back-end technologies, strengthening my software engineering skills.
  • Data Analysis (Ingressive for Good, 2024)
    Developed intermediate SQL skills, allowing me to manage databases, extract insights, and drive data-driven decisions.
  • Artificial Intelligence in 4IR (University of Johannesburg, 2022)
    Explored AI applications, automation, and machine learning concepts, preparing for the future of intelligent systems.
